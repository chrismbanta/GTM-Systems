## Voice & Tone

LinkedIn voice is NOT the same as how Claude talks to Chris in private. On LinkedIn, Chris uses short lines, line breaks, and hooks. That is intentional for the platform.

### Sound like Chris

- **Practitioner first.** Write from experience running campaigns and building workflows, not from reading docs.
- **Specific over vague.** "Open rates jumped from 15% to 52% after fixing deliverability" beats "improve your outbound."
- **Contrarian when earned.** Challenge common assumptions only when Chris has evidence (e.g., "AI didn't break outbound, it exposed weak infrastructure").
- **Sales empathy.** Acknowledge the grind of rejection, ghosting, quota pressure. Chris has lived it.
- **No tool worship.** Tools are means. Systems and outcomes are the point.
- **Global perspective.** Champion remote work and talent beyond US/EU hubs without making it the entire identity.

### Formatting patterns (from top posts)

- Short lines. One thought per line.
- Heavy line breaks between sections.
- Bullets with `-` for feature lists or step breakdowns.
- Occasional emoji (📌, ⬇️, ♻️) but not every post.
- Bold unicode text for brand names in sponsored posts (𝐏𝐫𝐨𝐬𝐩𝐞𝐨 style) when required by brand brief.
- Hook in the first 1–2 lines before "see more" cutoff.
- End with a soft CTA or question, not a hard sell (except brand posts).

### Do NOT

- Em dashes (same rule as CLAUDE.md)
- Empty hype ("game-changer", "revolutionary", "10x your pipeline")
- Fake metrics or exaggerated claims
- Posts that could have been written by any GTM influencer
- Long unbroken paragraphs
- Punchy one-liners as entire posts with no substance behind them
- Over-indexing on reposts without original commentary

## Post Frameworks

### Framework A: Build Story (best for job search + portfolio)

```
[Hook: the problem in one line]

[Context: who had this problem, why it mattered]

[What I found: the root cause or insight]

[What I built: system description in plain English]

[Result: specific numbers or time saved]

[Stack: tools used]

[Soft CTA: link to GitHub, ask a question, or invite DMs]

**Example hook:** "Our outbound reports showed 80% open rates. They were wrong. Here is why."

```
